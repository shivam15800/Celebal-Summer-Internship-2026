# Overall Insights

* The Superstore dataset analysis revealed significant variation in customer purchasing behavior and sales contribution.
* A small group of customers generated a large portion of total revenue, while several customers contributed only minimal sales.
* SQL techniques such as Subqueries, CTEs, and Window Functions were effective in identifying high-value customers, customer rankings, and sales patterns.
* Customer sales analysis showed that not all customers contribute equally to business growth, highlighting the importance of customer segmentation and retention strategies.
* The use of ranking and aggregation functions provided valuable insights into customer performance and helped identify both top-performing and low-performing customers.





## Conclusion



This assignment demonstrated how SQL can be used to transform raw sales data into meaningful business insights. By applying Subqueries, Common Table Expressions (CTEs), Joins, and Window Functions, customer sales trends, rankings, and purchasing behavior were successfully analyzed. The results can support business decision-making by identifying valuable customers, improving customer retention efforts, and understanding revenue distribution across the customer base.
